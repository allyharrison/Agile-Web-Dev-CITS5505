Lulu La Delizia

97 Rokeby Rd, Perth Western Australia 6008

Lulu La Delizia is a small eatery and wine bar specialising in the premium fresh pasta and the delights of Friuli and the Venetian spice route. Named in honour of our Nonna, Luigia Valvasori, who emigrated to Australia in 1952 from the town of Casarsa della Delizia, Friuli, in the far North-East of Italy, bringing with her the wonderful culinary traditions that make up the heart and soul of our creations.

Balthazar

6 The Esplanade, Perth WA 6000

A European inspired restaurant, tucked partly underground, situated at the base of an original art-deco apartment block, the 'Lawson' apartments. A pocket of calm, the mood is relaxed and refined – celebrating everyday with thoughtful dishes focusing on local produce. To complement our food, our large selection of wines takes you on a journey, focusing on terroir driven and minimal intervention.

Fleur at The Royal

531 Wellington St, Perth WA 6000

Nestled within the grand, heritage-listed structure of The Royal, fleur is as bold as she is delicate, as contemporary as she is classic. fleur invites you to delight in dishes showcasing local produce of incomparable quality, through a delicate French lens, brought to you by way of genuine, tailored service with a smile… and a chat should you fancy. You’ll find our carefully curated wine list reads like our soundtrack: interesting, eclectic, and, first and foremost, designed to be enjoyed. A dining experience created equally for the midweek indulgence and the celebratory occasion dinner.

Si Paradiso

1/446 Beaufort St, Highgate WA 6003

The cellar is made up of lo-fi, natural-leaning wines from Italy and Western Australia. The cocktail list is big on Campari and reimagined Negronis, Americanos and other interwar classics. Tap beer is available with the state’s craft brewing community well represented. Then there's the puffy and pliable pizza; a golden crust laden with a red sauce of San Marzano tomatoes, and topped with options such as salumi, confit garlic and good cheese.

40 Marine Parade, Cottesloe WA 6011

With knockout views and an elevated offering unlike anything in Perth, George Kailis' swanky new South Cott brasserie has already cemented its status as one of Perth's most premium dining destinations.

Bertie

77 Old Perth Rd, Bassendean WA 6054

Secret’s out—we’re a tiny bit in love with Bassendean's sleek little wine bar slash restaurant, Bertie. With an ex-Vue De Monde chef at the helm, this gem is all about fancying up British-inspired classics like the humble scotch egg, pork terrine and steak and chips.

Cooee

If you're chasing sophistication and riverside ambience, you can't go wrong with Cooee. Grab a seat outside and get stuck into the new autumn menu crafted by Culinary Manager Thomas Haynes and Head Chef, Caio Matiussi. From cauliflower and cheese croquettes and lobster rolls piled high with the freshest WA catch to Linley Valley pork belly with white beans and Tuscan kale, we're obsessed with these modern Australian delights. Don't be afraid to throw a bowl of their hand-cut rosemary salt fries on too!
